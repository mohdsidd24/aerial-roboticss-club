// Mission

Build. Fly. Compete.

We're a student-founded, multidisciplinary engineering team building autonomous UAS platforms capable of object detection, tracking, and precision payload delivery.

No prior drone experience needed. Just a drive to build things that actually fly — and actually work under pressure.

  • Full aircraft design and fabrication lifecycle
  • On-campus 3D printing for rapid prototyping
  • Real autonomous systems: CV, GPS, flight control
  • Precision payload delivery system
  • Compete nationally in SUAS
// What We Build

Aircraft Models

We design and fabricate multiple aircraft types, each serving a distinct mission profile. All vehicles are built for the SUAS competition framework.

MODEL 01

Quadcopter UAS

Custom-built quad for hovering object detection and precision payload delivery. Equipped with GPS, computer vision, and autonomous flight.

Primary · SUAS
MODEL 02

Fixed-Wing Aircraft

Hand-launched airframe for long-endurance missions. Optimised for extended search area coverage and loitering over search zones.

Secondary · Endurance
MODEL 03

Object Detection & Payload Drop

Onboard computer vision pipeline identifies and classifies ground markers in real time. Precision payload delivery system drops on operator command.

Mission Critical · SUAS
MODEL 04

R&D Experimental

Long-cycle research projects — custom flight controllers, new airframe materials, AI-based autonomous object classification, and novel delivery mechanisms.

Research · 2+ yr
// Sub-Teams

Find Your Role

Every member joins a focused sub-team based on their major and interests. No experience required — bring your curiosity.

Mechanical Eng

Airframe & Structures

CAD design in SolidWorks, FEA simulation, 3D printing, CNC, composites. You design the wings, body, and landing gear.

Electrical Eng

Avionics & Power

PCB design, motor/ESC selection, wiring harnesses, power systems, sensor integration (IMU, GPS, barometer, lidar).

Computer Science

Software & Autonomy

ArduPilot / PX4 stack, ROS2 nodes, computer vision object detection with OpenCV/YOLO, ground control station development.

Computer Eng

Embedded Systems

Flight controller firmware, payload control microcontrollers, real-time telemetry, hardware-software integration.

Mechanical Eng

Propulsion & Payload

Motor sizing, propeller selection, power-to-weight optimisation, payload delivery mechanisms, and actuation systems.

All Majors

Systems Integration

Test planning, subsystem integration, documentation, competition strategy. The glue that makes the whole aircraft work.

// Focus Competition

Compete Nationally

Year one is laser-focused on one competition — the gold standard of collegiate UAS.

SUAS — Student Unmanned Air Systems

The gold standard of collegiate UAS competition. Autonomous flight, real-time object detection, classification, and precision payload delivery. Hosted by AUVSI and run by the US Navy. Our sole focus for Year 1.

// Year-1 Roadmap

Launch Plan

From zero to competition-ready in twelve months.

Aug – Sep 2026

Phase 01 — Founding

Register club officially, recruit 15–20 charter members, elect leadership, secure faculty advisor and lab space.

Sep – Oct 2026

Phase 02 — Setup

Purchase starter components, set up GitHub org and project tooling, establish weekly build sessions, onboard all sub-teams.

Oct 2026 – Jan 2027

Phase 03 — First Build

Complete first full drone build. Maiden flight. Integrate CV pipeline. Begin payload delivery system testing via 3D printer.

Jan – Apr 2027

Phase 04 — Competition Prep

Register for SUAS 2027, submit design report. Refine aircraft, integrate payload delivery system, run full mission simulations.

May – Jun 2027

Phase 05 — Compete

Compete nationally. Present results to university. Debrief, document, and begin Year 2 recruitment cycle.
